The scenario

Why this topic matters

AI agents are about to transact on behalf of consumers at scale. Visa MPP, Mastercard CTF and Google AP2 already exist. The question is which Australian issuers move from "card on file" to "agent-ready credential" first, and who captures the new fee pools.

Opportunity
"First mover writes the dispute rules."
  • Tokenisation fee capture on a brand new transaction class
  • Behavioural data on agent decisioning to fund underwriting
  • Set refund and dispute norms that lock in incumbent advantage
Problem
"Stranded at the back of the value chain."
  • Fintechs intermediate the wallet layer and capture economics
  • One mishandled agent chargeback collapses consumer trust
  • Issuer brand relegated to a dumb funding source, no comeback
Context
"The cement is still wet."
  • Visa MPP live since 2025; Mastercard CTF in two-issuer pilot
  • Google AP2 specs landed late 2025; RBA review pending
  • 18 to 24 month window before liability and rails fully lock

  • Form an internal agent-commerce squad spanning product, risk and legal; report to ExCo monthly

Convert pilot into a defensible position as the agent-credential issuer of choice
  • Migrate 60% of card-on-file relationships to agent-ready tokens via in-app prompts
  • License the credential SDK to mid-tier issuers; capture acquiring economics on their volume
